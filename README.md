# Working With AI — My Principles

> AI should amplify human expertise, not replace it. Its value is not in producing answers faster — it lies in helping people ask better questions, explore better alternatives, and build better solutions.

## Why I Use AI

AI is a working tool. I use it the same way I use any other professional instrument — to get specific tasks done faster and with better quality. It helps me explore ideas, analyze alternatives, sharpen communication, and accelerate implementation.

It does not replace critical thinking, professional judgment, or accountability. Whether I am analyzing business processes, designing information architectures, building reporting systems, or writing software, the responsibility for the final outcome is mine.

**AI supports my work. It never replaces my responsibility.**

## My Philosophy

Technology should support better decisions, not make decisions for people. The same principle applies to AI.

Good solutions begin with understanding the business problem. AI is one of the tools I use during that process — the same way I use Excel or Power BI. It is never the source of truth.

## How I Work

AI accelerates implementation. It does not replace solution design.

When I build a reporting system, an Excel model, or a software solution, I follow the same sequence:

1. Understand the business objective.
2. Analyze the processes and information involved.
3. Design the overall solution.
4. Use AI to accelerate implementation.
5. Validate the result manually.
6. Document the solution for future maintenance.

This order matters. When AI is used before the problem is understood, the output looks polished but solves the wrong thing.

## Where I Use AI

AI is part of my daily work across several areas:

- **Business analysis** — structuring requirements, challenging assumptions, exploring alternative approaches.
- **Writing & communication** — drafting, editing, adapting content across languages.
- **Software development** — prototyping, code review, debugging, documentation.
- **Data & reporting** — validating KPI definitions, designing spreadsheet models, discussing analytical approaches.

Across all of these, AI output is treated as a suggestion — never a conclusion.

## Verification

I do not assume AI-generated information is correct. Before anything reaches a deliverable, I check what matters in that context: factual claims, calculations, technical accuracy, logical consistency, business relevance. When accuracy is critical, I go to authoritative or primary sources.

## Confidentiality

I do not share client-confidential information, proprietary business data, credentials, or personal sensitive information with AI tools. When discussing real projects, I anonymize business details.

## What I Won't Do With AI

I don't use AI to fabricate evidence, impersonate anyone, deceive clients, or produce information I know to be misleading. Speed and efficiency are never reasons to bypass professional responsibility.

## When I Disclose AI Use

I disclose AI involvement when it is required by contract or policy, expected in academic work, requested by a client, or when transparency adds real value to the reader.

Routine assistance — editing, brainstorming, language refinement, programming support — does not normally require explicit disclosure unless agreed otherwise.

### Attribution examples

**Short**
AI tools assisted with research, drafting, editing, and implementation support. All outputs were reviewed, validated, and approved by the author.

**Standard**
AI tools supported this work through brainstorming, technical exploration, drafting, editing, and software development assistance. All outputs were critically reviewed and validated before publication. The author accepts full responsibility for the final work.

**Detailed**
AI tools were used throughout this project for idea generation, business analysis, writing, programming, and language refinement. AI accelerated parts of the implementation process but did not replace professional judgment or decision-making. All outputs were reviewed for factual accuracy, technical correctness, and business relevance before publication. No confidential information was intentionally disclosed. The author retains full responsibility for every recommendation and every published result.

## Continuous Learning

AI evolves quickly, and so does my approach to using it. I revisit these principles regularly to make sure AI continues to *improve* the quality of my work — not dilute it.
